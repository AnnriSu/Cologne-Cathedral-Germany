About Cologne Cathedral

Cologne Cathedral by Omar Ramadan on Unsplash

Cologne Cathedral, or Kölner Dom, is a stunning example of Gothic architecture located in Cologne, Germany. Construction began in 1248 and was completed in 1880, spanning over six centuries. The cathedral is renowned for its impressive twin spires, intricate stained glass windows, and remarkable interior design. It houses numerous art treasures and religious relics, including the Shrine of the Three Kings. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Cologne Cathedral attracts millions of visitors annually, serving as both a place of worship and a symbol of cultural heritage.
